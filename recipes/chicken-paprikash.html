Chicken Paprikash

Description

Chicken Paprikash is a dish of Hungarian origin, the name coming from the large amount of paprika that defines the dish. It is a simple dish and one with many regional varieties. This page will feature my family's recipe, which can be served with egg noodles or mashed potatoes.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons salt
  • 1 tablespoon paprika (normal or sweet)
  • 1/2 teaspoon hot paprika
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 2 tablespoons flour
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 cup thinly chopped onion
  • 1-5 lbs of chicken (boneless, skinless)

Steps

  1. Take out milk and sour cream
  2. Slice onion into small, thin pieces
  3. Heat oil in large pot
  4. Saute onion until soft and translucent
  5. Add spices and mix with onions
  6. Add chicken to pot, covering pieces in sauce mixture
  7. Saute chicken until all sides are no longer pink
  8. Once all chicken is cooked, add water until chicken is covered
  9. Cover pot and simmer on low for 30-35 minutes
  10. Once done cooking, remove chicken into separate bowl with sauce remaining in pot
  11. Combine flour, milk, and sour cream in bowl. Whisk to eliminate lumps.
  12. Bring pot to gentle boil and poor in sour cream mixture, stirring until broth thickens
  13. Reduce pot heat and simmer for 5 minutes
  14. Replace chicken once time is up on sauce
  15. Serve with your choice of side, and enjoy!
